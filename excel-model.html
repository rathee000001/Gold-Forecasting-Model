3. Regression Output (What It Means)

The regression estimates how month-end macro and risk variables relate to the gold price level. The statistics below summarize overall fit, model significance, and which drivers are statistically reliable.

Model fit (Regression Statistics):

  • R Square: 0.7986 (‚âà 80% of variation in gold price explained in-sample)
  • Adjusted R Square: 0.7911 (fit remains strong after accounting for 6 predictors)
  • Observations: 168 monthly points
  • Standard Error: 140.1577 (typical residual size in USD/oz units)

Overall model significance (ANOVA):

  • F-statistic: 106.402
  • Significance F: 2.04E-53 (the model is statistically significant as a whole)

Key coefficient takeaways (direction + significance):

  • US10Y Nominal: negative and highly significant ‚Üí higher yields increase opportunity cost of holding gold.
  • CPI: positive and highly significant ‚Üí inflation pressure supports gold as a store of value.
  • USD Index: negative and significant ‚Üí stronger USD typically weighs on gold prices.
  • VIX: negative and significant in this sample ‚Üí captures risk regime effects in the training period.
  • CB_Gold: negative and significant here ‚Üí indicates a measurable structural-demand channel in the model dataset.
  • GPR: not statistically significant in this regression (p ‚âà 0.141), so its effect is weaker/less stable in-sample.

Important note: Even with strong in-sample fit, out-of-sample performance can degrade after 2020 due to structural breaks (COVID stimulus, inflation regime shift, geopolitics, central bank behavior changes).
