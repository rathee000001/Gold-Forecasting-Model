Backtest In-sample

This section reports the in-sample performance of the regression model during the training period. In-sample backtesting verifies that the model reproduces the historical relationships it was trained on before we evaluate performance on unseen data.

Training (In-sample) period: January 2006 – December 2019

What this test answers: “Does the model reproduce the historical macro–gold relationship within the training window?”

What it does NOT prove: Future accuracy. That is evaluated in the out-of-sample test (2020–2025).

Comparison used: Actual Gold Price (USD/oz) vs Model-implied price path (month-end aligned).

What the Chart Shows (Peaks, Regimes, and Gaps)

The purpose of the in-sample chart is to confirm that the model learns the main gold price regimes within 2006–2019. The key evaluation is whether the model follows the broad rise, peak period, correction, and later stabilization.

  • 2006–2008 gradual rise: Both series trend upward, indicating the model captures the early cycle and momentum in gold pricing.
  • 2008–2009 crisis transition: This region often shows short-run divergence because shocks move faster than monthly macro variables.
  • 2010–2012 peak regime: The chart reaches its highest zone; the model broadly follows the peak period, which is critical for validating fit.
  • 2013–2015 correction: Actual prices decline and stabilize lower; the model should also move downward (a sign it learned the regime shift).
  • 2016–2019 stabilization + renewed rise: The later part shows stabilization and recovery; the model’s ability to re-accelerate indicates sensitivity to changing macro conditions.

Note on spikes/gaps: Any sharp spikes in the model line usually indicate periods where one explanatory variable moved unusually fast (or where the regression amplifies a short-term move). This is why we report accuracy metrics (MAD/MSE/MAPE) separately below.

Interpretation: A MAPE of 9.22% means that, within the training window (2006–2019), the model’s predicted gold price level deviates from actual month-end prices by ~9% on average. This indicates the model captures the historical macro relationship well in-sample.

Decision use: Treat the in-sample backtest as a fit and calibration check. The real test of forecasting credibility is the out-of-sample period (2020–2025), where structural breaks can increase error.
