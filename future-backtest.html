Backtest Out-of-sample

This section evaluates true predictive performance on unseen data. The regression is trained on 2006–2019, then applied forward to Jan 2020 – Jun 2025 without re-training.

Out-of-sample (test) period: January 2020 – June 2025

What this test answers: “Does the model generalize when macro regimes change?”

Why this matters: Out-of-sample behavior is the best indicator of real forecasting credibility.

Analysis & Interpretation (Out-of-sample: 2020–2025)

How to read the chart: The blue line is the actual month-end gold price and the orange line is the model-implied predicted price path generated using coefficients trained on 2006–2019.

What typically happens in 2020–2025
  • Underprediction after major shocks: Post-2020 periods often show the model below actual price because the regime shift (COVID policy response, risk-on/risk-off swings) changes the historical mapping from macro variables to gold.
  • Peak timing vs peak magnitude: Linear macro models can often capture direction and turning points better than the exact height of extreme peaks (spikes are harder than trends).
  • Persistent gap after 2022: A widening gap is consistent with structural changes (inflation regime, geopolitical escalation, central bank reserve behavior) that are stronger than what the 2006–2019 training era contained.
How to explain “peaks” in your write-up
  • COVID shock (2020): Safe-haven demand and policy response can push prices beyond what a stable, pre-2020 linear relationship would imply.
  • Inflation + rate volatility (2021–2022): The model reacts through CPI and yields, but gold can also price uncertainty and policy credibility—effects not fully captured by the inputs.
  • Geopolitical risk (2022+): Conflict-driven risk premia may lift gold above “macro-only” levels; GPR helps, but magnitude can still be underestimated in extreme events.
Decision use (how you justify it for SCM)

Treat out-of-sample results as a realism check: even if the model misses peak levels, it can still be useful for direction, timing, and regime awareness (procurement windows, hedging triggers, risk monitoring). Larger errors are expected during structural breaks—this is an economic explanation, not an Excel mistake.

Conditional Forecasting Approach (2025–2026)

Purpose: The conditional forecast extends the estimated gold price model forward by holding macro-financial drivers at assumed or externally provided levels. This approach answers the question: “Given these macro conditions, what price level does the model imply for gold?”

Key distinction: This is not a free-running time-series forecast. Instead, it is a scenario-based conditional projection, where future gold prices are conditional on fixed assumptions for the input variables.

Methodology steps:

  1. The regression coefficients estimated from the historical training period are held constant.
  2. Future values for macro drivers (US 10Y nominal yield, CPI, USD index, geopolitical risk, VIX, and central bank gold holdings) are assumed to remain at their most recent observed or policy-consistent levels.
  3. The standardized inputs are passed through the regression equation to generate a predicted gold return.
  4. The predicted return is converted into a price level, producing a flat conditional price path across the forecast horizon.

Interpretation of the flat price path: Because all explanatory variables are held constant, the forecasted gold price remains stable across months. This does not imply zero uncertainty or no future movement, but rather reflects the model’s equilibrium-implied price under unchanged macro conditions.

Decision use: The conditional forecast should be interpreted as a baseline reference level. It is most useful for scenario comparison (e.g., “What happens to gold if real rates rise?”) rather than point prediction.
